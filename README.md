##Part B – Create a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist: 

# Social Media Do’s and Don’ts 

##  Do’s 
- Keep your profile clean and professional, especially if it can be viewed by teachers or recruiters
- Think before posting anything, as it can affect your image later
- Use privacy settings to control who can see your information
- Share useful, positive, or informative content instead of random or unnecessary posts
- Respect others’ opinions and communicate politely in comments and messages
- Be mindful of what you like, share, or comment on, as it also reflects your personality

## Don’ts - Don’t share personal or sensitive information like phone numbers, passwords, or location
- Don’t post offensive, inappropriate, or harmful content
- Don’t use abusive or rude language while interacting online
- Don’t click on or forward suspicious links, as they may be unsafe
- Don’t spread fake news or unverified information without checking
- Don’t overshare your daily life, which can affect your privacy and safety

#📄 CASE STUDY

# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where people are tricked into giving their personal details like passwords, bank information, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

A typical phishing attack usually starts with a message or email that looks real. For example, a person might receive a message saying their bank account will be blocked or that they have won a reward. The message includes a link and asks them to click on it urgently. When they open the link, it takes them to a fake website that looks very similar to the original one. Without realising it, the person enters their details, which are then collected by the attacker.

Students and young people are often targeted because they use online platforms regularly and may not always check if a link is genuine.

The result can be serious. Money can be stolen from bank accounts, accounts can be hacked, and personal information can be misused. This shows how important it is to be careful while using the internet.

#PREVENTION CHECKLIST

# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

- Always check who sent the message before clicking any link
- Never share your OTP, password, or bank details with anyone
- Try to use strong passwords that are not easy to guess
- Turn on two-factor authentication for extra security
- Avoid clicking on unknown or suspicious links

# UPI & Payment Safety 
- Do not accept unknown payment requests on UPI apps
- Always check the receiver’s name before sending money
- Avoid scanning QR codes from unknown sources
